Neuroplasticity & Growth Mindset

Neuroplasticity

Our brains can rewire themselves, literally changing in structure, making new connections and forming new neural pathways in order to learn new things. This ability is Neuroplasticity at work. And, curiously, our brains are actually better at doing so when we know this information.

Strange facts:

  • ⦁ Physical activity and good physical fitness can prevent or slow the normal age-related neuronal death and damage to the hippocampus, and even increase the volume of the hippocampus (Niemann et al., 2014);
  • ⦁ Chronic insomnia is associated with atrophy (neuronal death and damage) in the hippocampus, while adequate sleep may enhance neurogenesis (Joo et al., 2014).

Taking the above into account, the best things we can do for our own neural plasticity are to get enough sleep, exercise and then learn new things, especially things that require some physical activity like playing an instrument or learning a language (ie not just learning facts by rote).

Positive Psychology

Growth Mindset

Growth mindset is knowing that innate ability is not the only factor in success in a particular area. We can change our skills through effort, despite having no natural ability for a particular skill. It requires accepting that failure will happen but is an opportunity to learn; that the process of learning is the true goal, not the end result; that hard work is more valuable than inherant ability.

I think web development would be an impossibility without a growth mindset; there is no way that anyone could do it without making mistakes and I can't imagine too many people start out with a natural talent for it. Early on I realised I was going to have to accept that there were many errors to come and that the only way to learn how to do this new thing was to not accept that my ability is limited by anything other than my willingness to keep learning. So reading about growth mindset was quite reassuring; there is some research behind the conclusion I came to and it looks like it works!

'A person with a growth mindset believes that he or she can get smarter, better, or more skilled at something through sustained effort—which is exactly what neuroplasticity tells us. You might say that a growth mindset is simply accepting the idea of neuroplasticity on a broad level!' (positivepsychology.com n.d)
