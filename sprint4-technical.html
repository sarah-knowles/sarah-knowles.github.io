Problem Solving

I spent a lot of time getting stuck this week. I found the built-in methods kata very difficult. I am not familiar with the built in methods at all and I found them very hard to understand, despite spending time researching them. I had to check the sample solutions after several hours because I wasn't getting anywhere and the error messages weren't pointing me in the right direction (hopefully they will get easier to understand with practice).

The first difficult one didn't bother me too much, I was happy to wrestle with it for quite a while and wasn't getting too frustrated. Eventually I make some headway through trial and error. But when the next 5 were just as hard I started to lose focus and get cross with it. In the end I'm not sure I really learned very much at all. I will come back to it a few times in the next couple of weeks and see if I can get some of it to sink in.

Problem solving techniques and process:

  • Pseudocode - this is a handy way to sort out what you are actually trying to do, and makes the Googling part easier but I often don't know how to convert pseudocode into actual code.
  • Trying something - I end up doing this all the time. It's time consuming but I think I learn best this way, so it's time well spent in the end. It's a good feeling watching your code get closer and closer to what you want it to do.
  • Rubber ducky method - haven't tried it but I do talk to myself a bit.. would be worth a go, again telling the duck what you are doing makes you break everything down into simple chunks.
  • Reading error messages - occasionally make sense, mostly don't. Hoping to get better at picking meaning from them as I get to look at a lot of them...
  • Console.logging - I forget to do this as much as I could. A good way to check everything is working as expected as you go.
  • Googling - totally comfortable with this and spend a lot of time doing it. Essential, I'd never get anywhere without it. Thinking about good search terms is useful. Being as specific as possible gets the best results.
  • Asking your peers for help - I haven't done this yet and to be honest I don't feel all that comfortable doing it. It might be that I would be the first that I'm aware of to do so?
  • Asking coaches for help - also haven't got this stuck yet, although I got closer this week than I have to date.
  • Improving your process with reflection - Not an especially comfortable thing, certainly not a habit yet. It is not much fun to look at your day and think, well I got annoyed and none of what I did today has stuck in my head... So this week I have felt like not doing this. I understand that it helps the learning process though, so I've done as much as I could cope with.
